Passage:

Coral reefs, often referred to as the "rainforests of the sea," are some of the most diverse ecosystems on Earth. These underwater structures are formed by colonies of tiny animals called coral polyps, which secrete calcium carbonate to build protective skeletons. Coral reefs provide habitats for thousands of marine species, from colorful fish and sea turtles to sharks and sponges. In addition to their ecological importance, coral reefs benefit humans by acting as natural barriers against coastal erosion and storms, protecting shorelines and communities.

Despite their significance, coral reefs are under threat from human activities and environmental changes. Rising sea temperatures due to climate change are causing widespread coral bleaching, a phenomenon where corals expel the algae living in their tissues, leading to their whitening and potential death. Overfishing disrupts the delicate balance of reef ecosystems, while pollution from agricultural runoff and plastic waste further exacerbates the problem. Coastal development and destructive fishing practices, such as the use of explosives, also contribute to the degradation of these fragile ecosystems.

Efforts to protect coral reefs are underway worldwide. Marine protected areas, sustainable fishing regulations, and coral restoration projects aim to mitigate the damage and encourage recovery. Scientists are even exploring innovative techniques, such as breeding heat-resistant corals, to enhance reef survival in warming oceans. By addressing threats like climate change and pollution, these initiatives seek to preserve the invaluable biodiversity and ecological services provided by coral reefs for future generations.

Strategies to Find Specific Details

Finding specific details involves pinpointing key information within the text to answer precise questions or clarify understanding. These strategies will help you locate and interpret important details effectively:

What are Specific Details?

Specific details are the precise pieces of information provided in the text. They often serve as evidence or examples to support the main idea. Learning to identify these details ensures a thorough understanding of the text and helps answer detailed questions.

Strategies to Find Specific Details

Strategy 1: Focus on Keywords

Look for specific keywords or phrases in the question and match them to the text:

  • Names: Identify proper nouns, such as names of people, places, or organizations.
  • Dates and Numbers: Look for figures, timelines, or statistics.
  • Repetition: Notice words or ideas that are emphasized multiple times.
Strategy 2: Skim and Scan

Use targeted reading techniques to locate specific information quickly:

  • Skim: Read rapidly to get an overview of the passage.
  • Scan: Look for the particular word, phrase, or concept in the text.
  • Highlight: Mark the relevant sections for closer analysis.
Strategy 3: Pay Attention to Signal Words

Signal words or phrases often indicate key details:

  • For Example: Highlights supporting details.
  • Specifically: Introduces precise information or examples.
  • In Contrast: Indicates opposing details or comparisons.
Strategy 4: Use Context

Context helps narrow down the search for specific information:

  • Paragraph Structure: Details often follow the main idea of the paragraph.
  • Text Features: Italics, bold text, or bullet points can highlight key details.
  • Question Clues: Use the wording of the question to guide your search.
Strategy 5: Cross-Reference the Passage

Ensure accuracy by cross-referencing specific details in the passage:

  • Compare Statements: Verify details by checking adjacent sentences.
  • Fact-Checking: Ensure details match the text exactly.
  • Avoid Assumptions: Focus on what the text explicitly states.
Strategy 6: Practice with a Detailed Example

Launched in 1977, Voyager 1 and Voyager 2 provided groundbreaking data about Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. These missions revealed the presence of volcanic activity on Jupiter's moon Io and confirmed the existence of faint rings around Neptune. Even today, the Voyager spacecraft continue to transmit data as they journey beyond our solar system.
