Strategies to Understand Vocabulary in Context

Learning to determine the meaning of unfamiliar words using context clues is an essential reading skill. These strategies will guide you to uncover the meaning of words without a dictionary:

What is Vocabulary in Context?

Vocabulary in context refers to understanding the meaning of a word based on the surrounding text. This skill helps readers decode unfamiliar words by examining how they are used in a sentence or paragraph.

Strategies to Understand Vocabulary in Context

Strategy 1: Look for Synonyms or Definitions Nearby

Authors often provide clues to a word's meaning in the same sentence or nearby sentences:

  • Definitions: Look for phrases like "which means" or "that is" to find a direct explanation.
  • Synonyms: Words with similar meanings might be placed nearby to clarify the term.
Strategy 2: Use Contrast Clues

Sometimes, the author uses contrasting words or ideas to highlight the meaning:

  • Antonyms: Words like "but," "however," or "unlike" can signal opposite meanings.
  • Comparisons: Phrases that show differences can help deduce meaning.
Strategy 3: Pay Attention to Word Parts

Breaking down a word into its components can provide valuable hints:

  • Prefixes: Identify common prefixes (e.g., "un-" for not, "pre-" for before).
  • Roots: Look for familiar word roots that suggest the core meaning.
  • Suffixes: Recognize suffixes that indicate word forms (e.g., "-ly" for adverbs, "-tion" for nouns).
Strategy 4: Examine Context Clues

Analyze the broader context of the sentence or paragraph to infer meaning:

  • Tone: Consider whether the tone is positive, negative, or neutral to guide your interpretation.
  • Examples: Examples or lists in the text often provide hints.
  • Situations: The scenario described can give clues about the word's meaning.
Strategy 5: Reread and Reflect

Sometimes the meaning becomes clear after rereading the sentence or paragraph:

  • Restatement: Reread and paraphrase the sentence to see if it clarifies the word's meaning.
  • Reassess: Think about how the word fits the overall idea of the text.
Strategy 6: Practice with a Detailed Example
