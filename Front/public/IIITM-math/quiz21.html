📐 Continuity Answer Formatting Guide

How to type your answers in the answer box:

  • Continuity at a point: Type continuous at x=2 or discontinuous at x=0
  • Type of discontinuity: removable discontinuity, jump discontinuity, infinite discontinuity
  • Interval notation: (-∞,2) ∪ (2,∞) or [0,5] or x ≠ 1
  • Condition for continuity: f(a) = lim(x→a) f(x) or limit exists and equals function value
  • Making a function continuous: k = 3 or k = -2 or c = 5
  • Continuity on interval: continuous on [-2,3] or continuous everywhere
  • Piecewise functions: continuous for all real x or discontinuous at x=1

Examples of correctly formatted answers:

continuous at x=0
→ For a function that is continuous at x=0
removable discontinuity at x=3
→ For a hole that can be filled
k = 4 or k=4
→ Value to make function continuous
(-∞,1) ∪ (1,∞)
→ Domain of continuity (all except x=1)

Important Tips:

  • Use ∞ for infinity (type 'infinity' or use ∞ symbol)
  • Use U for union of intervals
  • Use → or -> for limits if needed
  • Check the preview below the input box to see your formatted answer
