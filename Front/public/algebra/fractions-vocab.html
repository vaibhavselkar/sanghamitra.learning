Essential Words for Learning Fractions

Number

A number is a basic unit of mathematics used to count, measure, and label.

Example: 1, 2, 3, 4, and so on.

Whole

A whole refers to something complete or not divided. It is the entire object or unit.

Example: A whole apple.

Part

A part is a portion or division of a whole. It is less than the whole.

Example: Half of an apple.

Equal

Equal means the same in quantity, size, or value.

Example: Two equal halves of a bar of chocolate.

Divide

To divide means to split something into parts or groups.

Example: Dividing a cake into 4 pieces.

Numerator

The numerator is the top number in a fraction, indicating how many parts are being considered.

Example: In ¾, the numerator is 3.

Denominator

The denominator is the bottom number in a fraction, showing the total number of equal parts.

Example: In ¾, the denominator is 4.

Fraction

A fraction is a way to represent parts of a whole using two numbers.

Example: ½ means one part out of two.

Equivalent

Equivalent means having the same value or amount, even if the form is different.

Example: ½ is equivalent to ²⁄₄.

Simplify

To simplify a fraction means to reduce it to its smallest form.

Example: ⁶⁄₈ simplifies to ¾.

Add

To combine numbers or quantities together.

Example: Add ¼ and ½ to get ¾.

Subtract

To take away a number or quantity from another.

Example: Subtract ⅓ from ¾ to get ⁵⁄₁₂.

Percentage

A percentage is a way to express a number as a part of 100.

Example: 50% is the same as ½.

Multiply

To increase a number by another number or factor.

Example: Multiply ⅔ by 3 to get 2.

Improper Fraction

A fraction where the numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator.

Example: ⁷⁄₄ is an improper fraction.

Mixed Fraction

A whole number combined with a fraction.

Example: 1 ½ means 1 whole and ½ of another.

Reciprocal

The reciprocal of a fraction is created by swapping its numerator and denominator.

Example: The reciprocal of ⅔ is ³⁄₂.

Ratio

A ratio compares two quantities, showing how much of one thing there is compared to another.

Example: A ratio of 2:3 means 2 apples for every 3 oranges.

Proportion

A proportion shows that two ratios or fractions are equal.

Example: ½ is proportional to ²⁄₄.

Least Common Multiple (LCM)

The smallest number that is a multiple of two or more numbers.

Example: The LCM of 4 and 6 is 12.

Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

The largest number that divides exactly into two or more numbers.

Example: The GCF of 8 and 12 is 4.

Common Denominator

A shared denominator among two or more fractions, making it easier to compare or add them.

Example: ¼ and ⅙ have a common denominator of 12.

Proper Fraction

A fraction where the numerator is less than the denominator.

Example: ¾ is a proper fraction.

Unit

A standard measurement or a single quantity.

Example: A centimeter is a unit of length.
