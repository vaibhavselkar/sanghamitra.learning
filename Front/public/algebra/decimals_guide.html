Decimals Guide

An interactive guide to help students understand decimal concepts.

Understanding place value is crucial in decimals:

11 = 1 × 10 + 1 × 1
56 = 5 × 10 + 6 × 1

Example of Addition:

11 + 56 = 1 × 10 + 1 × 1 + 5 × 10 + 6 × 1
= 1 × 10 + 5 × 10 + 1 × 1 + 6 × 1
= (1 + 5) × 10 + (1 + 6) × 1
= 6 × 10 + 7 × 1
Result: 67

The decimal point separates the whole number part from the fractional part of a number.

Example: In 24.36, the decimal separates 24 (whole) from 36 (fractional).

The term "deci" in decimals refers to powers of 10. Here are some examples:

Examples:

  • 1/10 = 0.1
  • 1 + 1/10 = 1.1
  • 1/100 = 0.01
  • 1 + 1/100 = 1.01

Using properties like commutative and distributive properties makes decimal addition straightforward:

1.1 + 5.6 = (1 × 1 + 1 × 0.1) + (5 × 1 + 6 × 0.1)
= 1 × 1 + 5 × 1 + 1 × 0.1 + 6 × 0.1 (commutative property)
= (1 + 5) × 1 + (1 + 6) × 0.1 (distributive property)
= 6 × 1 + 7 × 0.1
Result: 6.7

In practice, it's easy to add decimals by aligning place values:

Operations:
  • Hundredths: 0 + 2 = 2 (write down 2)
  • Tenths: 3 + 9 = 12 (write down 2, carry over 1 to units place)
  • Units: 4 + 8 + 1 = 13 (write down 13)

Multiplying by 10 shifts the decimal one position to the right.

Examples:

  • 1.0 × 10 = 10.0
  • 10.0 × 10 = 100.0
  • 0.1 × 10 = 1.0
  • 0.01 × 10 = 0.1
  • 23.25 × 10 = (2 × 10 + 3 × 1 + 2 × 0.1 + 5 × 0.01) × 10
    = 2 × 10 × 10 + 3 × 1 × 10 + 2 × 0.1 × 10 + 5 × 0.01 × 10
    = 2 × 100 + 3 × 10 + 2 × 1 + 5 × 0.1
    Result: 232.5

Dividing by 10 shifts the decimal one position to the left.

Examples:

  • 1.0 ÷ 10 = 0.1
  • 10.0 ÷ 10 = 1.0
  • 100.0 ÷ 10 = 10.0
  • 0.1 ÷ 10 = 0.01
  • 78.64 ÷ 10 = (7 × 10 + 8 × 1 + 6 × 0.1 + 4 × 0.01) ÷ 10
    = 7 × 10 ÷ 10 + 8 × 1 ÷ 10 + 6 × 0.1 ÷ 10 + 4 × 0.01 ÷ 10
    = 7 × 1.0 + 8 × 0.1 + 6 × 0.01 + 4 × 0.001
    Result: 7.864

Round to the nearest decimal place based on the following digit.

Rules:

  1. If rounding up to the nth decimal place, look at the (n+1)th decimal place.
  2. If the value in the (n+1)th decimal place is:
    • Less than 5, drop all values after the nth decimal place.
    • Greater than or equal to 5, add one to the nth decimal place and drop all values after.

Example: Round 3.456 to 3.46

Compare the whole number portion first, then decimal places if equal.

Fractions can be represented as decimals for easier comparison.

Example: ½ is the same as 0.5

Test your understanding with the following:

Problem: Add 4.3 + 8.92

Solution: 13.22
