Decimal Point

The decimal point separates the whole number part from the fractional part in a decimal.

Example: In 3.14, the decimal point separates 3 from 14.

Tenths

The first place to the right of the decimal point, representing parts out of ten.

Example: In 0.3, the 3 is in the tenths place.

Hundredths

The second place to the right of the decimal point, representing parts out of a hundred.

Example: In 0.05, the 5 is in the hundredths place.

Thousandths

The third place to the right of the decimal point, representing parts out of a thousand.

Example: In 0.007, the 7 is in the thousandths place.

Rounding

The process of adjusting a number to a specified place value to make it simpler.

Example: 3.142 rounded to two decimal places is 3.14.

Place Value

The value of a digit based on its position in a number.

Example: In 45.67, 4 is in the tens place, and 6 is in the tenths place.

Whole Number

A number without fractions or decimals, a complete unit.

Example: 5 in 5.2 is a whole number part.

Decimal Fraction

A fraction represented in decimal form, rather than as a fraction.

Example: 0.5 represents ½.

Equivalent Decimals

Decimals that represent the same value, though they may look different.

Example: 0.50 and 0.5 are equivalent decimals.

Convert

To change a number from one form to another, such as from a fraction to a decimal.

Example: ¼ can be converted to 0.25.

Repeating Decimal

A decimal that has a digit or group of digits that repeats endlessly.

Example: 0.333... is a repeating decimal.

Terminating Decimal

A decimal that ends after a finite number of digits.

Example: 0.25 is a terminating decimal.
