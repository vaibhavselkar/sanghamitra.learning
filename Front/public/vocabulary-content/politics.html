Democracy

/dəˈmäkrəsē/
noun
Definition: a system of government by the whole population or all the eligible members of a state, typically through elected representatives.
Synonyms: republic self-government self-rule
Antonyms: dictatorship autocracy
Examples:
  • The principles of democracy ensure that the government is elected by the people.
  • Many nations aspire to have a democratic system of governance.
  • Democracy promotes the idea of freedom and equality for all citizens.

Republic

/rəˈpəblik/
noun
Definition: a state in which supreme power is held by the people and their elected representatives, and which has an elected or nominated president rather than a monarch.
Synonyms: commonwealth democracy self-government
Antonyms: monarchy autocracy
Examples:
  • The United States is a republic where the president is elected by the citizens.
  • In a republic, the head of state is typically not a monarch.
  • The Roman Republic was one of the earliest examples of a republic.

Autocracy

/ôˈtäkrəsē/
noun
Definition: a system of government by one person with absolute power.
Synonyms: dictatorship tyranny despotism
Antonyms: democracy republic
Examples:
  • The country was ruled by an autocracy for decades.
  • Autocracy often leads to the suppression of political dissent.
  • Under autocracy, power is concentrated in the hands of a single ruler.

Oligarchy

/ˈäləˌɡärkē/
noun
Definition: a small group of people having control of a country, organization, or institution.
Synonyms: plutocracy junta aristocracy
Antonyms: democracy republic
Examples:
  • The nation was governed by an oligarchy of wealthy families.
  • Oligarchy can lead to corruption and unequal distribution of wealth.
  • The company's board of directors operated like an oligarchy.

Monarchy

/ˈmänərkē/
noun
Definition: a form of government with a monarch at the head.
Synonyms: kingdom empire sovereignty
Antonyms: republic democracy
Examples:
  • The United Kingdom is a constitutional monarchy.
  • The monarchy has a long history in European countries.
  • In a monarchy, the monarch's power can be absolute or ceremonial.

Federalism

/ˈfed(ə)rəˌlizəm/
noun
Definition: the federal principle or system of government.
Synonyms: unionism centralism
Antonyms: separatism localism
Examples:
  • Federalism allows states to have certain powers while being part of a larger union.
  • The United States operates under a system of federalism.
  • Debates on federalism often focus on the balance of power between national and state governments.

Socialism

/ˈsōSHəˌlizəm/
noun
Definition: a political and economic theory of social organization which advocates that the means of production, distribution, and exchange should be owned or regulated by the community as a whole.
Synonyms: collectivism communalism
Antonyms: capitalism individualism
Examples:
  • Socialism aims to reduce economic inequality by redistributing wealth.
  • Many countries have adopted elements of socialism in their economic policies.
  • The principles of socialism advocate for public ownership of resources.

Capitalism

/ˈkapədlˌizəm/
noun
Definition: an economic and political system in which a country's trade and industry are controlled by private owners for profit, rather than by the state.
Synonyms: free enterprise market economy
Antonyms: socialism communism
Examples:
  • Capitalism encourages competition and innovation in the marketplace.
  • Critics argue that capitalism can lead to economic inequality.
  • Capitalism is the dominant economic system in many countries around the world.

Communism

/ˈkämyəˌnizəm/
noun
Definition: a political theory derived from Karl Marx, advocating class war and leading to a society in which all property is publicly owned and each person works and is paid according to their abilities and needs.
Synonyms: Marxism socialism
Antonyms: capitalism democracy
Examples:
  • The principles of communism advocate for a classless society.
  • Communism aims to eliminate the disparity between the rich and the poor.
  • The Soviet Union was one of the most prominent examples of a communist state.

Liberalism

/ˈlib(ə)rəˌlizəm/
noun
Definition: a political and social philosophy that promotes individual rights, civil liberties, democracy, and free enterprise.
Synonyms: progressivism libertarianism
Antonyms: conservatism authoritarianism
Examples:
  • Liberalism advocates for equal opportunities and rights for all citizens.
  • Many political movements in history have been influenced by liberalism.
  • Liberalism supports the idea of a free and open society.

Conservatism

/kənˈsərvəˌtizəm/
noun
Definition: a political and social philosophy that promotes retaining traditional social institutions and values.
Synonyms: traditionalism right-wing
Antonyms: liberalism progressivism
Examples:
  • Conservatism emphasizes the importance of preserving cultural heritage.
  • Many conservative policies aim to maintain established social norms.
  • Conservatism often advocates for limited government intervention in the economy.

Populism

/ˈpäpyəˌlizəm/
noun
Definition: a political approach that strives to appeal to ordinary people who feel that their concerns are disregarded by established elite groups.
Synonyms: grassroots demagoguery
Antonyms: elitism establishment
Examples:
  • Populism often emerges during times of economic or social crisis.
  • Many populist leaders claim to represent the voice of the common people.
  • Populism can be found across the political spectrum, both on the left and right.

Authoritarianism

/ôˌTHôritəˈrizəm/
noun
Definition: a form of government characterized by strong central power and limited political freedoms.
Synonyms: dictatorship totalitarianism autocracy
Antonyms: democracy liberalism
Examples:
  • Authoritarianism often involves the suppression of political opposition.
  • Many authoritarian regimes maintain control through censorship and propaganda.
  • The rise of authoritarianism is often accompanied by a decline in civil liberties.

Nationalism

/ˈnaSH(ə)nəˌlizəm/
noun
Definition: an ideology that emphasizes loyalty, devotion, or allegiance to a nation or nation-state and often holds that such obligations outweigh other individual or group interests.
Synonyms: patriotism j ingoism chauvinism
Antonyms: globalism internationalism
Examples:
  • Nationalism can inspire a strong sense of pride and unity among citizens.
  • Extreme nationalism can lead to xenophobia and conflict with other nations.
  • Many independence movements are fueled by nationalist sentiments.

Globalism

/ˈɡlōbəˌlizəm/
noun
Definition: the operation or planning of economic and foreign policy on a global basis.
Synonyms: internationalism cosmopolitanism
Antonyms: nationalism isolationism
Examples:
  • Globalism promotes cooperation and understanding between nations.
  • Critics of globalism argue that it can undermine national sovereignty.
  • Many international organizations advocate for policies based on globalism.

Sovereignty

/ˈsäv(ə)rən(t)ē/
noun
Definition: supreme power or authority; the authority of a state to govern itself or another state.
Synonyms: autonomy independence self-governance
Antonyms: subordination dependence
Examples:
  • National sovereignty is a key principle in international relations.
  • Many countries fought for their sovereignty and independence.
  • Sovereignty allows a state to create and enforce its own laws.

Diplomacy

/dəˈplōməsē/
noun
Definition: the profession, activity, or skill of managing international relations, typically by a country's representatives abroad.
Synonyms: negotiation statesmanship foreign affairs
Antonyms: belligerence hostility
Examples:
  • Diplomacy is crucial for maintaining peaceful international relations.
  • Skilled diplomacy can resolve conflicts without resorting to war.
  • Many countries rely on diplomacy to build alliances and partnerships.

Impeachment

/imˈpēCHmənt/
noun
Definition: a charge of misconduct made against the holder of a public office.
Synonyms: indictment accusation charge
Antonyms: exoneration acquittal
Examples:
  • The impeachment of the president was a major political event.
  • Impeachment proceedings are usually initiated by the legislature.
  • Impeachment does not necessarily result in removal from office.

Bureaucracy

/byo͝oˈräkrəsē/
noun
Definition: a system of government in which most of the important decisions are made by state officials rather than by elected representatives.
Synonyms: administration red tape civil service
Antonyms: anarchy disorganization
Examples:
  • The bureaucracy often faces criticism for being inefficient and slow.
  • Bureaucracy is essential for the implementation of government policies.
  • Many citizens are frustrated by the complexities of bureaucracy.
