Atom

/ˈætəm/
noun
Definition: the basic unit of a chemical element, consisting of a nucleus (containing protons and usually neutrons) and one or more electrons.
Synonyms: particle elementary particle
Antonyms: molecule compound
Examples:
  • An atom of oxygen consists of eight protons.
  • Carbon atoms are essential for the formation of organic molecules.

Gravity

/ˈɡrævɪti/
noun
Definition: the force that attracts a body towards the center of the earth, or towards any other physical body having mass.
Synonyms: gravitation attraction
Antonyms: levity weightlessness
Examples:
  • Objects fall to the ground due to gravity.
  • The moon's orbit around the Earth is governed by gravity.

Energy

/ˈɛnərdʒi/
noun
Definition: the capacity for doing work, producing light, heat, or other forms of power.
Synonyms: power force
Antonyms: inactivity lethargy
Examples:
  • Solar energy is harnessed through solar panels.
  • Chemical energy is released during a combustion reaction.

Cell

/sɛl/
noun
Definition: the smallest structural and functional unit of an organism, typically microscopic and consisting of cytoplasm and a nucleus enclosed in a membrane.
Synonyms: biological cell microscopic unit
Antonyms: organism macroscopic structure
Examples:
  • Plant cells contain chloroplasts for photosynthesis.
  • Animal cells lack a cell wall found in plant cells.

Photosynthesis

/ˌfoʊtəˈsɪnθəsɪs/
noun
Definition: the process by which green plants and some other organisms use sunlight to synthesize foods with the help of chlorophyll and release oxygen as a byproduct.
Synonyms: assimilation conversion
Antonyms: respiration decomposition
Examples:
  • Green plants perform photosynthesis to produce glucose.
  • Photosynthetic bacteria contribute to oxygen production in aquatic ecosystems.

Evolution

/ˌɛvəˈluːʃən/
noun
Definition: the gradual development of something, especially from a simple to a more complex form, or the process by which different kinds of living organisms are believed to have developed and diversified from earlier forms during the history of the Earth.
Synonyms: development progression
Antonyms: stagnation regression
Examples:
  • Charles Darwin proposed the theory of evolution through natural selection.
  • Evolutionary biology studies the mechanisms driving genetic changes over generations.

Fusion

/ˈfjuːʒən/
noun
Definition: the process or result of joining two or more things together to form a single entity.
Synonyms: merging integration
Antonyms: division separation
Examples:
  • Nuclear fusion is a process that powers stars.
  • Cell fusion plays a role in the formation of multi-nucleated cells.

Genetics

/dʒəˈnɛtɪks/
noun
Definition: the study of genes, genetic variation, and heredity in living organisms.
Synonyms: heredity genomics
Antonyms: environmental influence epigenetics
Examples:
  • Mendelian genetics explains the inheritance of traits from parents to offspring.
  • Genetic engineering allows for the modification of organisms' genetic material.

Microscope

/ˈmaɪkrəˌskoʊp/
noun
Definition: an optical instrument used for viewing very small objects, such as microorganisms or fine structures that are not visible to the naked eye.
Synonyms: optical instrument magnifying glass
Antonyms: telescope binoculars
Examples:
  • Antonie van Leeuwenhoek is credited with developing the first practical microscope.
  • Electron microscopes allow for the study of structures at a molecular level.

Plate Tectonics

/pleɪt tɛkˈtɒnɪks/
noun
Definition: a theory explaining the structure of the Earth's lithosphere, which is divided into several large plates that float on the semi-fluid asthenosphere and interact with one another at their boundaries.
Synonyms: continental drift geological movement
Antonyms: stability stationarity
Examples:
  • Plate tectonics explain earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, and the formation of mountain ranges.
  • The theory of plate tectonics was developed in the mid-20th century.

Replication

/rɛplɪˈkeɪʃən/
noun
Definition: the process by which genetic material, typically DNA, is copied to produce two identical replicas.
Synonyms: duplication copying
Antonyms: deletion elimination
Examples:
  • DNA replication is essential for cellular growth and division.
  • Viral replication involves the production of new viral particles within host cells.

Hydrogen Bond

/ˈhaɪdrədʒən bɒnd/
noun
Definition: a weak electrostatic bond between a hydrogen atom and another electronegative atom, such as oxygen or nitrogen, that occurs in molecules containing polar covalent bonds.
Synonyms: polar bond dipole-dipole interaction
Antonyms: covalent bond ionic bond
Examples:
  • Hydrogen bonds contribute to the unique properties of water, such as high surface tension.
  • Protein folding is influenced by hydrogen bonds between amino acid residues.

Acid

/ˈæsɪd/
noun
Definition: a chemical substance that has a pH value lower than 7, typically capable of reacting with a base to form a salt, and turning litmus paper red.
Synonyms: sour substance corrosive substance
Antonyms: base alkali
Examples:
  • Hydrochloric acid is a strong acid used in industrial processes.
  • Citric acid is found naturally in citrus fruits and gives them a tart taste.

Mass

/mæs/
noun
Definition: a measure of the amount of matter in an object, usually expressed in kilograms or grams.
Synonyms: weight density
Antonyms: volume lightness
Examples:
  • The mass of an object determines its gravitational attraction to other objects.
  • In physics, mass is a fundamental property used to calculate momentum.

Molecule

/ˈmɒlɪkjuːl/
noun
Definition: the smallest unit of a chemical compound that can exist, consisting of two or more atoms held together by covalent bonds.
Synonyms: particle compound
Antonyms: atom element
Examples:
  • Water is a molecule composed of two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom.
  • Complex molecules such as proteins play essential roles in biological functions.

Neutron

/ˈnjuːtrɒn/
noun
Definition: a subatomic particle found in the nucleus of an atom, with no electrical charge and a mass slightly larger than that of a proton.
Synonyms: atomic particle nuclear particle
Antonyms: proton electron
Examples:
  • Neutrons play a crucial role in maintaining atomic stability and determining isotopes.
  • Neutron stars are incredibly dense celestial objects composed almost entirely of neutrons.

Reproduction

/ˌriːprəˈdʌkʃən/
noun
Definition: the biological process by which organisms produce offspring, involving the transmission of genetic material from parent to offspring.
Synonyms: breeding procreation
Antonyms: sterility infertility
Examples:
  • Sexual reproduction involves the fusion of gametes from two parents.
  • Asexual reproduction produces genetically identical offspring through mitosis.
