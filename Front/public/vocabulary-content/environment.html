Environment Vocabulary

Ecology

/iˈkäləjē/
noun
Definition: the branch of biology that deals with the relations of organisms to one another and to their physical surroundings.
Synonyms: environmental science ecosystem study
Antonyms: pollution environmental damage
Examples:
  • Ecology helps us understand the natural world and our place in it.
  • She decided to study ecology in college.

Sustainability

/səˌsteɪnəˈbɪlɪti/
noun
Definition: the ability to be maintained at a certain rate or level, especially without causing environmental degradation.
Synonyms: conservation eco-friendliness
Antonyms: exploitation depletion
Examples:
  • Sustainability is key to the future of our planet.
  • They implemented sustainability practices in their business model.

Biodiversity

/ˌbīōdīˈvərsədē/
noun
Definition: the variety of life in the world or in a particular habitat or ecosystem.
Synonyms: biological diversity species richness
Antonyms: monoculture uniformity
Examples:
  • Biodiversity is essential for ecosystem resilience.
  • The rainforest is known for its rich biodiversity.

Climate Change

/ˈklaɪmət ʧeɪnʤ/
noun
Definition: a change in global or regional climate patterns, particularly a change apparent from the mid to late 20th century onwards, attributed largely to the increased levels of atmospheric carbon dioxide produced by the use of fossil fuels.
Synonyms: global warming climatic shift
Antonyms: climate stability natural climate
Examples:
  • Climate change is one of the biggest challenges facing humanity today.
  • Efforts to combat climate change are underway globally.

Deforestation

/ˌdiːfɒrɪˈsteɪʃən/
noun
Definition: the action of clearing a wide area of trees.
Synonyms: logging clear-cutting
Antonyms: reforestation afforestation
Examples:
  • Deforestation has significant impacts on the environment.
  • Efforts are being made to reduce deforestation and promote reforestation.

Pollution

/pəˈluːʃən/
noun
Definition: the presence in or introduction into the environment of a substance or thing that has harmful or poisonous effects.
Synonyms: contamination impurity
Antonyms: purity cleanliness
Examples:
  • Pollution is a major environmental issue affecting air, water, and soil.
  • Efforts to reduce pollution are crucial for environmental health.

Recycling

/rɪˈsaɪklɪŋ/
noun
Definition: the action or process of converting waste into reusable material.
Synonyms: reuse reprocessing
Antonyms: waste disposal
Examples:
  • Recycling helps reduce waste and conserve resources.
  • Many communities have recycling programs in place.

Renewable Energy

/rɪˈnjuːəbəl ˈɛnərdʒi/
noun
Definition: energy from a source that is not depleted when used, such as wind or solar power.
Synonyms: green energy sustainable energy
Antonyms: nonrenewable energy fossil fuels
Examples:
  • Renewable energy sources are crucial for reducing carbon emissions.
  • Solar panels and wind turbines are examples of renewable energy technologies.

Conservation

/ˌkɒnsəˈveɪʃən/
noun
Definition: the action of conserving something, in particular, preservation, protection, or restoration of the natural environment and wildlife.
Synonyms: preservation protection
Antonyms: destruction neglect
Examples:
  • Conservation efforts are essential for maintaining biodiversity.
  • She works in wildlife conservation.

Habitat

/ˈhæbɪˌtæt/
noun
Definition: the natural home or environment of an animal, plant, or other organism.
Synonyms: environment home
Antonyms: uninhabitable area desert
Examples:
  • The forest is the natural habitat of many species.
  • Habitat destruction is a major cause of biodiversity loss.

Greenhouse Effect

/ˈɡriːnˌhaʊs ɪˈfɛkt/
noun
Definition: the trapping of the sun's warmth in a planet's lower atmosphere due to the greater transparency of the atmosphere to visible radiation from the sun than to infrared radiation emitted from the planet's surface.
Synonyms: climate warming atmospheric warming
Antonyms: cooling global cooling
Examples:
  • The greenhouse effect is a natural process that warms the Earth’s surface.
  • Human activities have intensified the greenhouse effect, leading to global warming.

Ozone Layer

/ˈoʊzoʊn ˈleɪər/
noun
Definition: a layer in the earth's stratosphere at an altitude of about 10 km (6.2 miles) containing a high concentration of ozone, which absorbs most of the ultraviolet radiation reaching the earth from the sun.
Synonyms: ozone shield protective layer
Antonyms: ozone depletion UV exposure
Examples:
  • The ozone layer protects life on Earth by absorbing harmful UV radiation.
  • Efforts to reduce CFCs have helped protect the ozone layer.

Carbon Footprint

/ˈkɑrbən ˈfʊtˌprɪnt/
noun
Definition: the amount of carbon dioxide and other carbon compounds emitted due to the consumption of fossil fuels by a particular person, group, etc.
Synonyms: carbon emissions environmental impact
Antonyms: carbon reduction carbon offset
Examples:
  • Reducing your carbon footprint can help combat climate change.
  • Many companies are working to minimize their carbon footprint.

Fossil Fuels

/ˈfɑsəl ˌfjuəlz/
noun
Definition: a natural fuel such as coal or gas, formed in the geological past from the remains of living organisms.
Synonyms: coal oil natural gas
Antonyms: renewable energy green energy
Examples:
  • Fossil fuels are a major source of energy worldwide.
  • Burning fossil fuels releases greenhouse gases into the atmosphere.

Organic Farming

/ɔrˈɡænɪk ˈfɑrmɪŋ/
noun
Definition: a method of farming that uses natural processes and inputs to enhance soil fertility and control pests, rather than synthetic chemicals.
Synonyms: natural farming eco-farming
Antonyms: conventional farming industrial agriculture
Examples:
  • Organic farming practices promote soil health and biodiversity.
  • Many consumers prefer products from organic farming.

Greenwashing

/ˈɡriːnˌwɑːʃɪŋ/
noun
Definition: disinformation disseminated by an organization so as to present an environmentally responsible public image.
Synonyms: environmental disinformation eco-deception
Antonyms: transparency authentic sustainability
Examples:
  • Greenwashing can mislead consumers about the environmental benefits of a product.
  • It's important to be aware of greenwashing tactics.

Composting

/ˈkɒmpɒstɪŋ/
noun
Definition: the process of recycling organic waste, such as food scraps and yard waste, into a rich soil amendment called compost.
Synonyms: organic recycling soil amendment
Antonyms: waste landfill
Examples:
  • Composting is an effective way to reduce household waste.
  • She started composting her food scraps to create nutrient-rich soil for her garden.

Emission

/ɪˈmɪʃən/
noun
Definition: the production and discharge of something, especially gas or radiation.
Synonyms: discharge release
Antonyms: absorption capture
Examples:
  • Carbon dioxide emissions contribute to climate change.
  • Regulations are in place to control industrial emissions.

Ecosystem

/ˈiːkəʊˌsɪstəm/
noun
Definition: a biological community of interacting organisms and their physical environment.
Synonyms: biome habitat
Antonyms: urban area city
Examples:
  • The coral reef is a diverse and vibrant ecosystem.
  • Human activities can disrupt natural ecosystems.

Renewable Resource

/rɪˈnjuːəbəl rɪˈsɔrs/
noun
Definition: a natural resource that can be replenished naturally with the passage of time, such as solar energy, wind energy, and biomass.
Synonyms: sustainable resource green resource
Antonyms: nonrenewable resource finite resource
Examples:
  • Renewable resources are essential for sustainable development.
  • Wind and solar power are examples of renewable resources.

Endangered Species

/ɛnˈdeɪndʒərd ˈspiːʃiːz/
noun
Definition: a species of animal or plant that is seriously at risk of extinction.
Synonyms: threatened species at-risk species
Antonyms: common species abundant species
Examples:
  • Conservation efforts are critical to protect endangered species.
  • The panda is an example of an endangered species.

Natural Resources

/ˈnætʃərəl rɪˈsɔrsəz/
noun
Definition: materials or substances such as minerals, forests, water, and fertile land that occur in nature and can be used for economic gain.
Synonyms: raw materials nature's bounty
Antonyms: man-made resources synthetic resources
Examples:
  • Natural resources are vital for economic development.
  • Proper management of natural resources is essential for sustainability.

Overpopulation

/ˌoʊvərˌpɑpjəˈleɪʃən/
noun
Definition: the condition of being populated with excessively large numbers.
Synonyms: overcrowding population explosion
Antonyms: underpopulation sparse population
Examples:
  • Overpopulation can lead to resource depletion and environmental degradation.
  • Policies to control overpopulation are necessary in some regions.
