Literature Vocabulary

Allusion

/əˈluːʒən/
noun
Definition: an expression designed to call something to mind without mentioning it explicitly; an indirect or passing reference.
Synonyms: reference mention hint
Antonyms: reality direct statement
Examples:
  • Her writing is full of allusions to classical literature.
  • The poem makes several allusions to the Bible.
  • His speech was filled with allusions to historical events.

Allegory

/ˈaləˌɡôrē/
noun
Definition: a story, poem, or picture that can be interpreted to reveal a hidden meaning, typically a moral or political one.
Synonyms: parable symbol metaphor
Antonyms: nonfiction literal
Examples:
  • The novel is an allegory of the Russian Revolution.
  • The film can be seen as an allegory of good versus evil.
  • His art is an allegory for the human condition.

Anachronism

/əˈnakrəˌnizəm/
noun
Definition: a thing belonging or appropriate to a period other than that in which it exists, especially a thing that is conspicuously old-fashioned.
Synonyms: misplacement chronological error prolepsis
Antonyms: modernity current
Examples:
  • The sword in the modern warfare movie was an anachronism.
  • The presence of a Victorian house in the middle of the city felt like an anachronism.
  • His love for handwritten letters in the digital age was charmingly anachronistic.

Archetype

/ˈärkəˌtīp/
noun
Definition: a very typical example of a certain person or thing.
Synonyms: prototype model standard
Antonyms: atypical unique
Examples:
  • He was the archetype of the successful businessman.
  • The hero's journey is a common archetype in literature.
  • Her character is an archetype of the wise old mentor.

Bildungsroman

/ˈbildo͝oNGzrōˌmän/
noun
Definition: a novel dealing with one person's formative years or spiritual education.
Synonyms: coming-of-age story development novel apprenticeship novel
Antonyms: biography nonfiction
Examples:
  • His debut novel is a bildungsroman about a young man's journey to self-discovery.
  • The book is considered one of the great bildungsromans of our time.
  • The protagonist's transformation in the bildungsroman was profound.

Catharsis

/kəˈTHärsəs/
noun
Definition: the process of releasing, and thereby providing relief from, strong or repressed emotions.
Synonyms: emotional release purgation cleansing
Antonyms: repression suppression
Examples:
  • Many people find crying to be a cathartic release.
  • The film's ending was a catharsis for the audience.
  • Writing in a journal can be a form of catharsis.

Dystopia

/disˈtōpēə/
noun
Definition: an imagined state or society in which there is great suffering or injustice, typically one that is totalitarian or post-apocalyptic.
Synonyms: anti-utopia nightmare hell
Antonyms: utopia paradise
Examples:
  • The novel is set in a dystopia where freedom is nonexistent.
  • She often wrote about dystopias to critique modern society.
  • The movie portrays a dystopia where technology controls every aspect of life.

Epiphany

/əˈpifənē/
noun
Definition: a moment of sudden revelation or insight.
Synonyms: revelation insight realization
Antonyms: confusion bewilderment
Examples:
  • During the meeting, he had an epiphany about how to solve the problem.
  • The character's epiphany changed the course of the story.
  • She experienced an epiphany that led her to pursue a different career.

Euphemism

/ˈyo͞ofəˌmizəm/
noun
Definition: a mild or indirect word or expression substituted for one considered to be too harsh or blunt when referring to something unpleasant or embarrassing.
Synonyms: understatement polite term substitute
Antonyms: dysphemism blunt term
Examples:
  • 'Passed away' is a euphemism for 'died'.
  • Using 'let go' instead of 'fired' is a common euphemism in business.
  • He prefers euphemisms when discussing difficult topics.

Foreshadowing

/fôrˈSHadōiNG/
noun
Definition: a warning or indication of a future event.
Synonyms: forewarning indication hint
Antonyms: surprise unpredictability
Examples:
  • The dark clouds were a foreshadowing of the storm to come.
  • The author used foreshadowing to build suspense in the story.
  • His early comments foreshadowed his later actions.

Hyperbole

/hīˈpərbəlē/
noun
Definition: exaggerated statements or claims not meant to be taken literally.
Synonyms: exaggeration overstatement embellishment
Antonyms: understatement litotes
Examples:
  • He used hyperbole to describe the size of the fish he caught.
  • Her claim that she could eat a horse was pure hyperbole.
  • The marketing campaign relied on hyperbole to generate interest.

Irony

/ˈīrənē/
noun
Definition: the expression of one's meaning by using language that normally signifies the opposite, typically for humorous or emphatic effect.
Synonyms: sarcasm satire mockery
Antonyms: sincerity seriousness
Examples:
  • It was a great irony that the fire station burned down.
  • The irony of the situation was lost on no one.
  • His voice was dripping with irony.

Juxtaposition

/ˌjəkstəpəˈziSHən/
noun
Definition: the fact of two things being seen or placed close together with contrasting effect.
Synonyms: contrast comparison collocation
Antonyms: similarity likeness
Examples:
  • The juxtaposition of light and dark in the painting was striking.
  • Her essay examined the juxtaposition of rural and urban life.
  • The film's juxtaposition of comedy and tragedy was masterful.

Metaphor

/ˈmetəˌfôr/
noun
Definition: a figure of speech in which a word or phrase is applied to an object or action to which it is not literally applicable.
Synonyms: figure of speech analogy comparison
Antonyms: literal simile
Examples:
  • She used a metaphor to describe her experience.
  • His poetry is rich in metaphors.
  • The metaphor of the heart as a pump is a common one in medicine.

Onomatopoeia

/ˌänəˌmatəˈpēə/
noun
Definition: the formation of a word from a sound associated with what is named (e.g., cuckoo, sizzle).
Synonyms: imitative harmony sound symbolism echoism
Antonyms: conceptual abstract
Examples:
  • Words like 'buzz' and 'murmur' are examples of onomatopoeia.
  • The poem's use of onomatopoeia made it more vivid.
  • Comic books often use onomatopoeia for sound effects.

Oxymoron

/ˌäksəˈmôrˌän/
noun
Definition: a figure of speech in which apparently contradictory terms appear in conjunction (e.g., faith unfaithful kept him falsely true).
Synonyms: paradox contradiction incongruity
Antonyms: pleonasm tautology
Examples:
  • His use of 'deafening silence' is a classic oxymoron.
  • She loves the oxymoron 'bittersweet'.
  • The phrase 'jumbo shrimp' is an oxymoron.

Paradox

/ˈperəˌdäks/
noun
Definition: a seemingly absurd or self-contradictory statement or proposition that when investigated or explained may prove to be well-founded or true.
Synonyms: contradiction inconsistency enigma
Antonyms: certainty truth
Examples:
  • His statement 'less is more' is a paradox.
  • The idea that time slows down at high speeds is a paradox of physics.
  • The paradox of his situation was that he needed to work less to achieve more.

Satire

/ˈsaˌtī(ə)r/
noun
Definition: the use of humor, irony, exaggeration, or ridicule to expose and criticize people's stupidity or vices, particularly in the context of contemporary politics and other topical issues.
Synonyms: parody caricature mockery
Antonyms: seriousness reverence
Examples:
  • The novel is a brilliant satire on the American political system.
  • His work is characterized by biting satire and sharp wit.
  • The film's satire was lost on those unfamiliar with the subject.

Symbolism

/ˈsimbəˌlizəm/
noun
Definition: the use of symbols to represent ideas or qualities.
Synonyms: representation signification emblematism
Antonyms: literalness directness
Examples:
  • The use of light and dark in the story serves as powerful symbolism.
  • The novel's symbolism adds depth to its themes.
  • Her art is rich in symbolism and meaning.

Theme

/THēm/
noun
Definition: the central topic, subject, or message within a narrative.
Synonyms: subject topic thesis
Antonyms: tangent digression
Examples:
  • The theme of the novel is the struggle for freedom.
  • Love and loss are recurring themes in his work.
  • The main theme of the story is resilience in the face of adversity.
